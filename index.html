What are MUSEcells™

Not just stem cells.
A different class entirely.

The treatments you've received at STEMS are built on mesenchymal stem cells (MSCs) — a proven, rigorously studied therapy that reduces inflammation, signals tissue repair, and produces real outcomes. That foundation hasn't changed. What has changed is what's now possible above it.

MUSEcells™ were discovered by Dr. Mari Dezawa at Tohoku University, Japan. MUSE stands for Multilineage-differentiating Stress Enduring. Once in the body, they find damaged tissue on their own — migrating autonomously via biological stress signals — and transform into the exact cell type needed for repair: nerve, muscle, cartilage, cardiac tissue, skin, liver, and more. They persist at the repair site for years. No immunosuppression required. Non-tumorigenic. Validated in international clinical trials.

Why IV Stem Cell Therapy Is Different

Beyond the injection site.
The body treated as a system.

The targeted treatments you know from STEMS are precise and effective — the right tool for site-specific repair. IV stem cell therapy operates from a different principle entirely. When cells are delivered intravenously, they enter the bloodstream and travel systemically — reaching every site of damage, inflammation, and cellular aging the body carries, simultaneously.

The question it answers is not just where it hurts. It is why the body is breaking down.

The key distinction

A targeted injection addresses the knee. IV stem cell therapy asks why the knee — and everything else — is breaking down in the first place. Both have a role. But only one has the potential to change the trajectory of your health long-term.

What IV therapy reaches that site-specific treatment cannot:

Common Questions

Is the lab FDA-registered and compliant?

Yes. FDA-registered, cGMP-compliant, operating under 21 CFR Part 1271. We work exclusively with labs that meet this standard — no exceptions.

How do I know the cells are alive and viable when injected?

Every batch is tested for cell viability before release, cryopreserved using validated protocols, and shipped under monitored cold-chain conditions. Cells are thawed precisely on the day of your procedure — not before.

Where do the cells come from, and how are donors screened?

Umbilical cord Wharton's jelly, donated by healthy mothers after planned caesarean births with full informed consent. Donors are screened for a full panel of infectious diseases — including HIV and hepatitis — per FDA requirements. Medical and family history reviewed. Only cleared donors accepted.

Has this been studied in clinical trials?

Yes. MUSEcells™ have been the subject of clinical trials in Japan and extensively studied in peer-reviewed literature across neurological, cardiovascular, autoimmune, and musculoskeletal indications. Dr. Choxi can speak to the evidence base specific to your condition.

Can I see the Certificate of Analysis for my batch?

Absolutely. A full CoA — documenting cell count, viability, sterility results, and identity markers for your specific lot — is available upon request. Always.
